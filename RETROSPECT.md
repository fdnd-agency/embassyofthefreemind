# Retrospect 18‐12‐2025

### Temperature Reading ([#22](https://retromat.org/en/?id=22))
Participants mark their 'temperature' (mood) on a flipchart
Prepare a flipchart with a drawing of a thermometer from freezing to body temperature to hot. Each participant marks their mood on the sheet.

### Why Retrospectives? ([#46](https://retromat.org/en/?id=46))
Ask 'Why do we do retrospectives?'
Go back to the roots and start into the retrospective by asking 'Why do we do this?' Write down all answers for everyone to see. You might be surprised.

### Positive and True ([#122](https://retromat.org/en/?id=122))
Boost everyone's energy with a tailored question
Source: [Veronika Kotrba and Ralph Miarka](http://www.twitter.com/sinnvollFUEHREN), adapted from [Nancy Kline](http://www.timetothink.com/meet-us/nancy-kline/)
Think of a question that is tailored to get a response that is positive, true and about their own experiences, e.g.
What have you done really well in the last iteration?
What is something that makes you really happy?
What were you most happy about yesterday?
Ask your neighbor the question. Then your neighbor asks their neighbor on the other side the same question and so on until everyone has answered and asked.

This will give everyone a boost and lead to a better retro.
